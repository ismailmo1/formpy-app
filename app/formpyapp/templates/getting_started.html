Quick Start Guide

Follow the 4 steps below to get started with formpy!

  • Decide how you want to structure your form as multiple choice questions, and then design your form in your preferred application (e.g. Excel, Word, Google Sheets). You can use the starter document template shown below to help structure your document.
  • Don't forget to add circles so your users know where to fill in the spots for their answers. (We used the ⃝ character in size 18 font)
  • Add a border around your form so formpy can use it for alignment when reading your completed forms
  • Convert your form into a pdf and upload in the create template page.

  • After you've uploaded a pdf of your template, formpy will detect the border (or add one for you) and add the alignment spots shown in red below. Confirm the spots are in the correct places and click on the green tick.
  • Next you will define the questions and answers for your template so formpy can understand your forms and knows where to look for answers when it reads the completed forms.
  • There's a lot going on here, so we'll do our best to explain!
    • The template settings include a title for your template (choose something descriptive so you remember what it was for), and a public toggle - this determines whether other users can use your template. You must have an account and be logged in to have non-public/private templates.
    • Formpy needs to understand where your answers are and what question they belong to. So add a questions and select it (question 1 is created and selected by default) before adding an answer circle and dragging it to the location on the form.
    • When formpy reads your forms it will determine which answer was selected and populate the table with the answer value. You can edit the answer value by clicking on the relevant circle.
  • Once all your questions and answers have been define, click on the save icon. Well done, that's the most tedious part over with!

  • Print your forms via the view templates page, ensure the borders are printed during the page setup before printing.
  • Step away from the digital realm and distribute your forms to start collecting data! Ensure the circles are filled in completely (we found that black markers work best for marking answers and silver markers to cover up mistakes). Note that formpy will not understand crossings out so they'll still be detected as answers.

  • The part we've been working towards! Once you've collected your responses, go to the read forms page.
  • Select your template, upload the scanned forms (they must be in pdf or image format) and click on read forms.
  • For each uploaded form, formpy will align the form, go through each question to find the answer(s) and then record the values in a table.
  • After this is complete, a table will appear with all your answers.
  • You can export this table as a csv and save it so you can share, analyse, do your thing etc.
  • Click on the the image icons to see the answers detected by formpy.
